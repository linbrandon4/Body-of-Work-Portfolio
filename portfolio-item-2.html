What is the relationship between the price of a property and its key attributes, such as the number of bedrooms, bathrooms, and square footage?

Los Angeles has a mean composite score of 8.08 and a median composite score of 7.036.

The procedure to find the composite score is outlined in the Portfolio Context

As seen below the scatter plot of the composite score as well as the corresponding cost of the housing in the listing.

Correl value between the listing price and the composite score resulted in 0.5229.

  • A value of 0.72 indicates a strong positive correlation between the Composite Number and Cost.
  • This means that as the Composite Number increases, the Cost tends to increase as well, but not perfectly (there's some variability).
  • A moderate correlation like 0.72 suggests that while the Composite Number explains the variation in Cost preey well, there are likely other small factors affecting price in Los Angeles.

This graph has a R2 value of 0.515, meaning that approximately 51.5% of the variation in Cost can be explained by the Composite Number. The remaining 48.9% of the variation is due to other factors that are not captured by the Composite Number.



Do listings with certain features on Redfin's website, like virtual walkthroughs or redesigns, correlate with higher prices?

After data cleaning out of 614 listings 10.59% are listed by Redfin, 9.12% have the 3D walkthrough feature, 20.01% have the Floor Plan feature, 100% have the Street View feature, and 1.31% have the Redesign feature.

After coducting a least squared test with the results above these results were found

  • The coefficient of Listed by Redfin means that holding other variables constant, properties listed by Redfin are associated with an average decrease in cost by $946,400. However, the p-value (0.000) suggests this is statistically significant.
  • The coefficient of 3D walkthrough means that properties with a 3D Walkthrough are associated with a increase in cost by $17900, on average, holding other variables constant. The high p-value (0.948) means this relationship is statistically insignificant.
  • The coefficient of Floor Plans means properties with a floor plan are associated with an average $105800 decrease in cost. The p-value (0.580) shows this relationship is not significant.
  • The coefficient of Street View menas that properties with a street view are associated with an average $1,601,000 increase in cost. The p-value (0.000) confirms this is also highly significant.
  • This means that, on average, properties marked with a redesign (when Redeisgn = 1) are associated with an increase in cost by $184,500 compared to properties without a redesign (when Redeisgn = 0), holding all other variables constant. The p-value is not significant (>0.05), meaning the observed increase in cost is statistically insignificant.
